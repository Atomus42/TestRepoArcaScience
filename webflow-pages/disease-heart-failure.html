Disease Overview

Why Heart Failure Demands Specialized BRA

Heart failure therapy has evolved into a complex multi-drug paradigm with four foundational pillars of GDMT (ARNI/ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i) plus emerging agents. Balancing efficacy across HFrEF and HFpEF subtypes while managing hypotension, renal decline, hyperkalemia, and device therapy integration creates unprecedented benefit-risk complexity.

GDMT Multi-Drug Regimen Optimization

The four pillars of GDMT — sacubitril/valsartan (PARADIGM-HF), beta-blockers, mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists, and SGLT2 inhibitors (DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced) — must be titrated simultaneously. Each agent carries additive risks of hypotension, bradycardia, hyperkalemia, and renal impairment. BRA must model drug-drug interactions and optimal sequencing strategies to achieve target doses while minimizing adverse events.

HFpEF Treatment Heterogeneity

Until EMPEROR-Preserved and DELIVER demonstrated SGLT2 inhibitor efficacy in HFpEF, no therapy had shown clear benefit in this phenotype. HFpEF encompasses diverse pathophysiologies — obesity-related, hypertensive, amyloid, and valvular — each with distinct risk profiles. BRA must account for phenotype-specific treatment responses and the challenge of applying trial data from selected populations to heterogeneous real-world patients.

Device Therapy & Transplant Integration

Advanced heart failure management integrates cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT), implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), left ventricular assist devices (LVADs), and cardiac transplantation with pharmacotherapy. BRA must evaluate the interplay between device-based and drug therapies, including anticoagulation management with LVADs, infection risks, and the benefit-risk calculus of transplant candidacy versus destination LVAD therapy.

Case Evidence — Heart Failure

SGLT2 Inhibitor Indication Expansion BRA for HFpEF

Challenge

A pharmaceutical company sought regulatory approval to expand their SGLT2 inhibitor from HFrEF to the full spectrum of heart failure including HFpEF, requiring comprehensive benefit-risk evaluation across heterogeneous patient populations with varying ejection fractions, comorbidity profiles, and concomitant GDMT regimens.

Result

ArcaScience's platform integrated DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced, EMPEROR-Preserved, and DELIVER trial data with real-world evidence to demonstrate consistent benefit-risk across the ejection fraction spectrum, identifying renal function as the key safety modifier and enabling a unified label that covered HFrEF through HFpEF with phenotype-specific dosing guidance.

SGLT2 inhibitors cause an initial eGFR dip that stabilizes and may provide long-term renoprotection, as demonstrated in DAPA-CKD and EMPA-KIDNEY. Our platform distinguishes this hemodynamic eGFR reduction from true nephrotoxicity using trajectory modeling that tracks eGFR slopes over time. This is critical for benefit-risk assessment because misinterpreting the initial dip as harm could lead to inappropriate drug discontinuation, while missing genuine renal decline in vulnerable populations could endanger patients.
