Gut-Selective Integrin Inhibitor

Moderate-to-Severe Ulcerative Colitis

Challenge

Required demonstration of improved infection safety profile vs. systemic immunosuppression while maintaining comparable mucosal healing rates

ArcaScience Approach

Built gut-selectivity BRA model comparing local vs. systemic immune suppression using integrated clinical trial and post-marketing infection surveillance data

Results

  • ✓Demonstrated 67% lower serious infection rate vs. anti-TNF therapies
  • ✓Validated gut-selectivity safety advantage across 12,000+ patient-years
  • ✓Generated FDA-accepted comparative safety analysis supporting labeling claims
2

Selective JAK1 Inhibitor

Moderate-to-Severe Crohn's Disease

Challenge

Post-ORAL Surveillance era required comprehensive cardiovascular and malignancy risk assessment specific to the IBD patient population

ArcaScience Approach

Developed IBD-specific cardiovascular risk model adjusting for chronic inflammation-mediated CV risk inherent to Crohn's disease patients

Results

  • ✓Distinguished JAK-related CV risk from IBD-inherent CV risk
  • ✓Identified IBD-specific risk-benefit threshold distinct from RA population
  • ✓Supported FDA supplemental NDA with population-specific BRA
3

Anti-IL-23 (p19) Antibody

Moderate-to-Severe Crohn's Disease

Challenge

Expanding from psoriasis/psoriatic arthritis indication required Crohn's-specific safety assessment across different dose regimens

ArcaScience Approach

Cross-indication BRA analysis leveraging 15,000+ patient-years of IL-23 inhibitor exposure data from dermatology and rheumatology programs

Results

  • ✓Validated consistent safety profile across dermatology and GI indications
  • ✓Optimized induction/maintenance dose based on cross-indication exposure-response analysis
  • ✓Reduced Crohn's BRA compilation time from 18 weeks to 14 days
