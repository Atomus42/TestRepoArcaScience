Technical Details

Data Sources

  • FAERS (FDA Adverse Event Reporting System): 10 years of spontaneous adverse event reports
  • EudraVigilance (EMA): European post-marketing surveillance data
  • PubMed & Embase: 2,400+ published studies on [THERAPEUTIC_AREA] and comparator therapies
  • Client Clinical Trial Database: Proprietary Phase I-III trial data with patient-level safety and efficacy endpoints
  • ClinicalTrials.gov: Public registration data for comparator therapies
  • Real-world evidence feeds: CPRD and Optum claims databases for safety signal validation

AI Models Applied

  • Signal Detection Models (n=6): Disproportionality analysis, temporal pattern recognition, multi-source signal corroboration
  • NLP Extraction Models (n=4): Unstructured case narrative processing, MedDRA auto-coding, adverse event extraction from literature
  • Causal Inference Models (n=3): Bradford Hill criteria evaluation, propensity score matching for comparator analysis
  • Risk Quantification Models (n=5): Incidence rate estimation, relative risk calculation, number-needed-to-harm derivation
  • Benefit-Risk Synthesis Models (n=6): MCDA weighting, scenario modeling, sensitivity analysis, lifecycle-aware recommendations

Validation Methodology

All ArcaScience analyses undergo multi-stage validation to ensure regulatory acceptability:

  • Data Quality Validation: Automated checks for data completeness, MedDRA coding accuracy, and cross-source deduplication
  • Model Output Review: Signal detection outputs reviewed by ArcaScience pharmacoepidemiologists to confirm clinical plausibility
  • Regulatory Alignment Check: All deliverables reviewed against FDA and EMA guidance documents for structured benefit-risk assessment
  • Client Expert Review: Final outputs reviewed by the client's medical and regulatory affairs teams before submission
  • Audit Trail: Complete data lineage tracking from source data to final conclusion, compliant with FDA 21 CFR Part 11

Regulatory Context

This benefit-risk assessment was conducted in alignment with:

  • FDA Guidance: "Benefit-Risk Assessment for New Drug and Biological Products" (2021)
  • EMA Guidance: "Benefit-Risk Methodology" and structured templates for benefit-risk assessment
  • ICH E2E: Pharmacovigilance Planning (PVPP) — signal detection and risk management integration
  • Submission Format: CTD Module 2.5 Clinical Overview with integrated benefit-risk summary
  • Outcome: FDA accepted all deliverables without major queries. No requests for additional benefit-risk analysis or reframing of safety signals.
